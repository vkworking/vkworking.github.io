FABULOUS CERAMICS VICTORIA AND VLADIMIR SHVETS
21.08.2014
VICTORIA SHVETS

The best work is creative work that brings income. And what can be better if in addition it is a family business. In this interview Madeheart.com will tell us an amazing story of one family, creative family, which lives in a “fairy tale” of modern ceramics. Vika Shvets was born in the town of Kosov, Ivano-Frankovsk region, she studied “Art ceramics” in Kosov College of folk art handicrafts, worked for some time at the Kosov Art Factory, until she quitted it in order to sharpen her skills and to create her own individual and inimitable works. Vika paints with water colors and makes ceramic things. Her husband Vladimir is also engaged in ceramics making. They develop ideas and create inimitable masterpieces together. For this couple it is not just a hobby but also a business that brings incredible pleasure. Their works are characterized with amazing joy of life and brightness.

Viktoria and Vladimir, when did you decide to make ceramic things?

— (Vika) My parents are ceramic artists but they work in traditional Ukrainian style. We were engaged in it from the very childhood, visited parents at work. We painted, molded, observed their work with curiosity. It was so interesting to create images and to bring them to life. I decided to develop in this direction – at first I studied in the art school and then graduated from Kosov Institute. In the course of time I sharpened my skill. I was looking for my style, individuality, in order my works to be inimitable and represent my view of the world!

Do you work together or separately? And where do you take your ideas from? Where do you take inspiration?

Yes, we work together. Making art works from ceramics is our family business. And as for inspiration – it is our outlook, our way of thinking, our observations. Sometimes inspiration comes just during a walk in nature – it is very interesting to watch nature and animals – their sincere behavior, instincts. We compare such observations with human habits, fill them with philosophical sense and humor. In this way we show people, their characters, personalities in our works.

Where did you take an idea of such unusual miniature molding and bright coloring?

It comes from our view of this world, our imagination and our personal outlook on life. Different people see the same thing in different ways. We interpret our outlook with the help of molding and painting. We see this world interesting and deep in bright and clear colors, not in dull ones. Sometimes it is difficult to implement an idea, sometimes you need to turn difficult job into easy one in order it can bring positive emotions.

How difficult is it to make such works and how much time does it take?

For me ceramics is such a material that on the one hand is easy to work with as it allows making any form you wish, and on another hand, work with this material requires high level of skill as any mistake will be visible. Any uncertainty in technique, in theme or just bad mood reflects on your work at once. The process itself is not very complicated. At first a figurine is molded from clay, then it is kilned and after cooling down it is painted with environmentally friendly Italian paints. And at the end of the process figurines are coated with water-proof varnish.
